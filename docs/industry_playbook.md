# Inventory Intelligence Playbook (Professional Baseline)

## 1. Common Industry Metrics
- Service level / fill rate
- Days on hand (DOH)
- Inventory turnover
- Reorder point (ROP)
- Safety stock
- Stockout rate
- Excess and obsolete ratio
- Supplier on-time in-full (OTIF)

## 2. Common Frameworks and Standards
- APICS/ASCM planning concepts (ROP, safety stock, lead-time management)
- SCOR process thinking (Plan, Source, Make, Deliver, Return)
- ABC/XYZ segmentation for differentiated control strategy
- S&OP and demand-supply balancing cadence
- Internal control principles: threshold governance, alert ownership, action tracking

## 3. Monitoring Layers
- Executive layer: inventory value, service-level trend, risk concentration
- Tactical layer: category/warehouse risk mix, supplier reliability, replenishment exceptions
- Operational layer: SKU-level gap, delayed receipt events, transaction anomalies

## 4. Alerting Governance
- Severity rules should be explicit and configurable
- Every alert should include owner, SLA, and close criteria
- Avoid static rules only: periodically recalibrate by seasonality and lead-time shifts

## 5. Attribution Principles
- Attribution must be evidence-driven from observed transaction and stock signals
- Distinguish correlation from causation; report confidence level and data sufficiency
- Provide benchmark comparisons and explain why a factor is dominant

## 6. Business Action Loop
- Detect -> Diagnose -> Prioritize -> Execute -> Verify impact
- Track KPI deltas after interventions (service level, DOH, shortage count, working capital)
